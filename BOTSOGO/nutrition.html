Nutrition & Healthy Eating

Fueling Our Bodies, Supporting Our Lives

Understanding Nutrition in Botswana

Nutrition is an essential part of health and wellness. In Botswana, traditional meals like bogobe, seswaa, and morogo are packed with natural nutrients that support immune strength and energy. The Botswana Ministry of Health promotes balanced diets that include local grains, vegetables, and lean protein.

Common Deficiencies and Prevention

  • Iron deficiency: Common among children and pregnant women. Prevent with dark leafy greens like morogo wa dinawa and red meat.
  • Vitamin A: Prevent by eating orange fruits/veggies like butternut and mangoes.
  • Fiber: Include sorghum, millet, and traditional beans to support digestion.

Sample Local Meal Plan

Traditional Healthy Meal Day

Breakfast: Sorghum porridge with milk & banana

Lunch: Seswaa (shredded beef), morogo (greens), and bogobe (maize meal)

Dinner: Grilled chicken, sweet potatoes, and a garden salad

Quick Tips for Eating Well

  • Drink at least 6–8 cups of water per day.
  • Limit processed sugar and salty snacks.
  • Use more fresh ingredients and less cooking oil.
  • Grow a small backyard garden for vegetables.
