Fitness & Movement

Simple ways to stay active and energized every day

Why Physical Activity Matters

Regular physical activity helps prevent diseases like diabetes, high blood pressure, and obesity. It improves mental health and boosts your energy levels. In Botswana, many people face sedentary lifestyles due to work and transport habits, making daily movement even more important.

Popular Local Activities

  • Walking Kgale Hill: A favourite weekend activity in Gaborone that promotes endurance and burns calories.
  • Community Aerobics: Held in parks and schools, this brings people together for fun, energetic sessions.
  • Traditional Dancing: Tsutsube and other dances offer great cardio while connecting to heritage.
  • Cycling: More youth are taking up cycling for both transport and health reasons in places like Maun and Francistown.

Exercise At Home

You don’t need a gym to stay active. Try these:

  • 10-minute jump rope sessions
  • Stretching and yoga in your living room
  • Push-ups and squats before or after school/work

Fitness Tips for Beginners

  • Start small and be consistent – even 10 minutes a day is progress.
  • Drink enough water before and after workouts.
  • Use a friend or family member as an accountability partner.
  • Don’t compare yourself to others — every journey is unique.

Global Perspective

According to the World Health Organization (WHO), adults should aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate aerobic activity weekly. Regular movement reduces the risk of depression, heart disease, and even some cancers.
